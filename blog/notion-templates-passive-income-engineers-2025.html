Low Effort

Why This Fits Engineers

A Notion template is a digital product with perfect margins — build once, sell infinitely. The skills it rewards are the ones engineers already have: systems thinking, clean structure, and solving a workflow problem cleanly. The hard part isn't building the template; it's marketing it. And that's exactly the part AI can now automate, which is what tips this from "nice idea" to "real income stream."

What Actually Sells

Templates that sell solve a specific, painful, recurring problem for a defined audience. Vague "life dashboards" are a crowded race to the bottom. Narrow, opinionated systems for a clear use case are where the money is. The best part: as an engineer, you already live inside several workflows worth productizing.

How to Build One Worth Paying For

1. Solve One Workflow Completely

Pick a single workflow and make the template handle it end to end. Don't build a sprawling everything-dashboard — build the best possible job-search tracker, or the best possible client CRM. Depth in one use case beats shallow coverage of ten. This is the same discipline as scoping a good MVP.

2. Make It Feel Designed

The difference between a free template and a paid one is polish. Clean layouts, sensible default views, helpful instructions, nice icons and covers. Engineers underrate this — but presentation is a huge part of perceived value. A template that looks professional commands a real price.

3. Use the Database Features

Relations, rollups, filtered views, formulas — these are what make a template feel like software instead of a glorified note. This is your home turf as an engineer. A buyer who couldn't build these themselves is exactly who pays for your template. Lean into the technical depth they can't replicate.

The Real Work: Marketing (Let AI Do It)

Here's the honest truth nobody tells you: building the template is 20% of the job. Getting people to find it is the other 80% — and that's where most engineers quit. The good news is that marketing is now the most automatable part, which flips the whole equation in your favor.

Play 03

Build a Catalog, Not a Product

One template is a trickle; ten related templates is a business. Each one cross-promotes the others, and a buyer of one becomes a buyer of the next. Engineers are good at building systems repeatably — apply that to your product line and the income compounds.
