"Passive income" is the most abused phrase in the side-hustle world. Almost nothing is truly passive — most of it is upfront work that pays out later. But engineers have an unfair advantage: we can build systems, and AI now does the parts that used to need a team. The honest framing isn't "make money in your sleep," it's "do the work once and let it compound." Here are seven realistic ideas, ranked from lowest effort to highest, with the AI tool stack and first step for each. I'm building several of these myself, so the rankings reflect real friction, not blog-post fantasy.

7 ideas

How These Are Ranked

Ranked by effort to first dollar, lowest to highest. Lower-effort ideas earn less per unit but start faster; higher-effort ones build bigger, more durable income but demand real upfront investment. Pick based on the time you actually have, not the income you wish you had.

The Seven Ideas

Idea 01 · Low Effort

Affiliate Content Around Tools You Already Use

The lowest-friction start: write honest reviews and guides for the tools you genuinely use, and earn recurring commission when readers sign up. As an engineer you have real opinions on developer and AI tools, which is exactly the credibility this needs. The trick is to write for tools with recurring affiliate programs so one good article keeps paying. The voice tool I use, ElevenLabs, pays recurring commission, as does the video platform Synthesia — both natural fits for an engineer's content stack.

Stack: a blog or static site · honest reviews · recurring affiliate programs. First step: write one review of a tool you already pay for.

Idea 02 · Low Effort

Sell Digital Templates & Packs

Notion templates, boilerplate repos, prompt packs, config starters — anything you'd build for yourself anyway, productized and sold once-to-many. The marginal cost of each sale is zero, which is what makes it genuinely scalable. Engineers sit on a goldmine here because the "tedious setup you've already solved" is exactly what others will pay to skip.

Stack: Gumroad or LemonSqueezy · a thing you already built · a landing page. First step: package one setup you've done twice into a downloadable.

Idea 03 · Medium Effort

Run a Faceless YouTube Channel

A scripted, faceless channel compounds: the back catalog keeps earning ad and affiliate revenue long after you publish. AI removes every excuse — an LLM writes the script, ElevenLabs narrates it, and Synthesia handles the on-screen presenter so you never touch a camera. The effort is consistency, not production. I cover the full build in the faceless YouTube channel guide.

Stack: LLM scripts · ElevenLabs voice · Synthesia video · a publishing schedule. First step: script and render one video this week.

Idea 04 · Medium Effort

Self-Publish Courses & Technical Content

Turn what you already know into a course that sells on repeat. The killer detail with AI is maintenance: when content goes stale, you fix one script and re-render a single lesson with Synthesia instead of re-filming a module. A focused technical course at a fair price is real recurring income with near-zero ongoing production cost once it's built.

Stack: course outline · Synthesia for video lessons · a course platform or Gumroad. First step: outline a course on something colleagues already ask you about.

Idea 05 · Medium Effort

Build Free Tools + Programmatic SEO

Build small free web tools — calculators, generators, graders — that rank in search and pull organic traffic you monetize with ads, affiliates, or an upsell. This is where the engineer advantage is widest: the "build the tool" step that stops everyone else is trivial for you, especially with an AI editor like Cursor doing the plumbing. I break down the build side in the Cursor AI guide for engineers.

Stack: Cursor for fast building · a static host · keyword-targeted pages. First step: ship one tool that solves a problem you've Googled.

Idea 06 · High Effort

Launch a Micro-SaaS

The highest ceiling and the highest effort: a small, focused product solving one narrow problem for a recurring fee. It's not passive while you build and support it, but a working micro-SaaS is the closest thing to a durable income asset on this list. AI coding tools like Cursor make shipping the MVP feasible on part-time hours — the hard part is finding a problem people will pay to solve and sticking with it.

Stack: Cursor for building · Stripe for billing · a narrow, painful problem. First step: ship a paid MVP to ten users before adding features.

Idea 07 · Capital-Gated

Dividend & Index Investing

The only genuinely passive option, and the one that needs capital instead of time. It belongs on this list because the income from the other six is best deployed here — a content engine or micro-SaaS that funds a compounding index position turns active hustle into truly passive growth. The other six make the money; this one makes it work while you sleep.

Stack: a brokerage · low-cost index funds · the income from ideas 1–6. First step: automate a recurring buy with whatever the other ideas earn first.

How to Actually Start

The mistake almost everyone makes is trying to run several of these at once and finishing none. Don't. Pick the single lowest-effort idea that fits your time this month, ship one unit of it — one review, one template, one video, one tool — and only then decide whether to go deeper or move on.

  1. Pick one idea from the table based on the hours you genuinely have.
  2. Ship one minimal unit of it this week — not a plan, an actual artifact.
  3. Let it run for a month and look at real numbers before adding anything.
  4. Reinvest whatever it earns into idea 07 so the work compounds.
